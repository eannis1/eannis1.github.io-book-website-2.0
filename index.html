At Your Fingertips

Using Free Online Tools to Study the Bible's Original Texts

This final instruction to be "perfect" has always felt heavy to us, especially given the radical instructions Jesus just gave. Loving those that you struggle to even tolerate is already and imposing challenge. It's like setting out to climb a mountain, only for Jesus to hand us a backpack full of rocks right before we start. Is Jesus really saying we must do these commands perfectly, without making any mistake, in any way? Maybe he is, because after all, God is flawless in all he does. And we should strive to be God's faithful representatives on earth. But what happened to "my yoke is easy, and my burden is light" (Matt. 11:30)?
